Streaming Customer Churn Analysis

A SQL and Power BI project analyzing subscriber churn for a streaming platform. The goal was to identify which customer segments, engagement patterns, and subscription types were most at risk, and to flag active subscribers who could still be saved through proactive retention efforts.

Portfolio Project 5,000 Subscribers Analyzed
SQL Power BI DAX

Project Overview

Retention analysis for a streaming platform

Customer churn directly threatens recurring revenue for subscription-based streaming platforms. This project investigates why subscribers leave and builds a way to flag at-risk customers before they cancel, so the business can act before revenue is lost rather than after.

Business Problem

Why the work mattered

Beyond the direct loss of subscribers, the greater risk is that customers who eventually churn often show warning signs beforehand — reduced engagement, long periods without logging in, or belonging to segments with historically higher churn rates. The business needed a way to catch these signals early enough to intervene.

Business Questions

Questions the analysis answers

🕑

Approach & Data Preparation

How the data was built and modeled

Created fields for engagement and time intelligence
Built the data model and DAX measures in Power BI

Key Insights

What the analysis revealed

Churn is a major revenue risk

Of 5,000 subscribers, 2,515 have churned — a 50.3% churn rate — linked to $33,009.85 in lost revenue.

Growing Customers churn the most

Growing Customers churn at ~57.96%, followed by Loyal Customers at ~53.18%. Long-Term (~38.67%) and New Customers (~35.93%) churn less.

Inactivity is a strong warning sign

Customers inactive for a month or more make up the largest churned group, at approximately 1,854 subscribers.

Basic subscribers churn the most by tier

Basic subscribers churn at ~40.95%, versus ~30.10% for Standard and ~28.95% for Premium.

Fewer profiles, higher churn

Accounts with one to three profiles churn at rates above 57%, while accounts with four or five profiles churn considerably less.

Churn moves over time

Monthly churn isn't constant — it peaks around January and March and dips around May and September.

615 active subscribers flagged for retention

These customers haven't churned yet, meaning targeted intervention could still protect future revenue.

Recommendations

Next steps for decision makers

Prioritise inactive customers

Place customers who haven't logged in recently into an automated re-engagement journey with personalised recommendations and incentives.

Focus on Growing Customers

Strengthen onboarding, content discovery, and engagement campaigns during the transition from new-user adoption to long-term use.

Target Basic subscribers

Investigate whether churn on the Basic plan stems from price sensitivity or limited perceived value, and test targeted offers.

Use risk segmentation to guide outreach

Give high-priority customers immediate, personalised outreach while continuing to monitor lower-priority segments.
